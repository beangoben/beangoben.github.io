Biography

I want to be an effective scientist, this means to me, contributing to solving challenging scientific problems that are impactful to other people, being a good mentor to other people, and actively trying to spread science.

I am Currently a graduate student at Harvard University with Alán Aspuru-Guzik @ The Matter Lab. My research centers around using machine learning techniques to 1) build data-driven models for prediction of molecular properties and 2) generation of new molecules and materials via generative models. Applications include solar cells, solubility, drug-design, and odorant molecules.

Education is a truly transformative experience, and so part of my time I dedicate to activities outside of research, two main projects of which I am a contributor and also founding member are “Clubes de Ciencia Mexico” and RIIAA, a summer school and ML conference in Mexico.
